# Duskers Market Note

> Date reviewed: 2026-05-20  
> Subject: [Duskers](https://store.steampowered.com/app/254320/Duskers/) by Misfits Attic  
> Purpose: summarize player reception, compare against Exergon's design, and extract practical lessons.

## Snapshot

Duskers is a drone-command survival roguelite about remotely piloting drones through derelict ships using limited sensors and a command-line interface. It is Exergon's strongest reference for drone-mediated exploration, imperfect information, and tension through distance.

At the time of review, Steam listed Duskers as `Very Positive`, with English reviews at 89% positive across more than 1,900 reviews and recent reviews `Very Positive` at 81%.

Useful source pages:

- Steam store: <https://store.steampowered.com/app/254320/Duskers/>
- Steam reviews: <https://steamcommunity.com/app/254320/reviews/>

## What Players Like

### Tension From Imperfect Information

Players love that motion sensors, door controls, drone cameras, audio, and sparse commands create fear without conventional horror excess. Not knowing exactly what is in the next room is the game.

### Drone Risk

Drones feel valuable because they are fragile, upgradeable, and expendable. Losing one changes future decisions.

### Diegetic Interface

The command-line style is not just presentation. It creates distance, friction, and commitment in a way that supports the fiction.

### Emergent Problem Solving

Players enjoy luring threats, routing drones, opening and closing doors, using ship systems, and improvising when plans fail.

### Atmosphere

Duskers' minimalism, audio, static, and loneliness are repeatedly praised. The restraint makes the world feel dangerous.

## What Players Dislike

### Interface Friction

Some players bounce off the command-line input, typing, and intentionally limited controls. What fans call immersion, others call clunkiness.

### Punishing RNG Or Loss

Bad ship layouts, scarce resources, or drone losses can feel harsh. The game accepts this as part of its roguelite identity, but it narrows the audience.

### Repetition

Derelict exploration can become repetitive once players understand common patterns.

### Limited Production Fantasy

Duskers is about survival and scavenging, not building. Its lessons transfer to exploration tone, not factory depth.

## Comparison To Exergon

| Axis | Duskers | Exergon |
|---|---|---|
| Core fantasy | Remote drone operator surviving derelicts | Scientist AI using drones to discover alien science |
| Structure | Roguelite survival expeditions | Run-based factory science campaign |
| Information | Limited sensors, imperfect knowledge | Fog of war, samples, partial reveals, remote scouting |
| Drone role | Primary body of play | Exploration instrument outside aegis fields |
| Failure | Drone/ship losses can spiral | Drone loss should sting, but runs remain completable |
| Interface | Command-line diegesis | Field computer, Local/Remote modes, planning tools |
| Main lesson | Distance and uncertainty create tension | Remote mode must feel risky and information-rich |

Duskers validates the idea that remote exploration can be emotionally powerful if information is limited and consequences are real.

## Lessons For Exergon

### 1. Remote Mode Needs Its Own Texture

Drone scouting should not feel like free camera movement. Limited sensors, range, signal, hazards, and physical drone risk should make Remote mode distinct from Local mode.

### 2. Information Is The Reward

In Duskers, a safe room or scanned threat is valuable knowledge. In Exergon, a sample, site, or observed phenomenon should directly affect the tech graph or production plan.

### 3. Do Not Copy Command-Line Friction Blindly

Duskers' interface works for its niche. Exergon needs tension without making factory players fight controls. Diegetic does not mean slow.

### 4. Drone Loss Should Hurt, Not End The Run

Losing a drone should cost resources, time, and access, but not create a 20-hour dead end.

### 5. Sound And Signal Design Matter

Remote exploration can be made tense through sensor noise, weak signal, warning tones, and partial visuals rather than enemies or combat.

## Store And Demo Implications

The demo should include one Remote-mode scouting trip where the player risks a drone to obtain information that changes the factory plan.

Potential positioning:

> Send fragile drones beyond the safe field to learn what your factory must become.

## Open Questions For Exergon

- What information can only be learned in Remote mode?
- How does a drone loss change the next 10 minutes?
- What is the minimum sensor model that creates tension without UI friction?
- How does scouting feed the graph viewer immediately?
