# Balance

> **Status:** Living methodology doc. Describes *how* balancing is done in Exergon and the open questions that drive it — **not** specific numbers. Specific quantities (yields, costs, thresholds, tier pacing) live in the `.ron` assets and are measured by the scenario runner; the current measured baseline is the generated [`balance-state.md`](balance-state.md).

---

## 1. Where the numbers live

**The `.ron` assets are the single source of truth for every balance number.** Recipe input/output quantities, processing times, energy costs, research amounts, production-milestone thresholds, node prerequisites — all of it is authored in `assets/` and read through the real (de)serializers (edit via the `exergon-assets` MCP, never by hand). Prose docs describe *structure and intent*; they do **not** restate quantities, because a number written twice drifts. If you want to know what a recipe costs, query the asset — do not read it from a design doc.

This is why the tier-by-tier content design lives holistically in [`tech-tree-design.md`](tech-tree-design.md) (tier themes, base/exotic ratios, unlock-rate targets, gates — §2, §3) and the **authored** RON spine in its §6bis, while the escape cascade is specced in [`technical/escape-condition.md §7.1`](technical/escape-condition.md#71-standard-escape-successor-launch). None of those carry live balance quantities as their authority — the RON does.

---

## 2. The evidence engine: the scenario runner

Balance is not argued in prose — it is **measured**. The scenario runner (`crates/scenario-runner`) executes a prescriptive `steps` list (`scenarios/*.ron`) against the real simulation and reports victory time, per-tier pacing, and DNFs.

- **`scenario balance <file> --seeds N`** (`crates/scenario-runner/src/balance.rs`) runs one baseline across N seeds and flags victory-time / tier-pacing outliers and did-not-finish runs. This is the primary tuning loop: change a number in the RON, re-run the sweep, read the effect.
- The current measured baseline per difficulty is emitted to [`balance-state.md`](balance-state.md), generated by `scenario balance --emit` (an auto-generated artifact — do not hand-edit it).

## 3. Reachability proof: the e2e tests

Separate from tuning, two end-to-end tests prove the run is *completable at all* (reachability, not balance):

- [`tests/standard_full_run.rs`](../tests/standard_full_run.rs) — a fully-earned landing → successor-launch Standard run, fast-forwarding simulated time, zero injected unlocks.
- [`tests/initiation_run.rs`](../tests/initiation_run.rs) — the Initiation minimal-successor run.

When a new stage lands on the victory path (research tier, crafting step, exploration unlock, escape), it gets a matching stage in the relevant test (see `technical/testing.md §3`). A green test means "the graph resolves to victory"; the balance sweep answers the separate question "does it resolve on a *reasonable schedule*."

## 4. Methodology — how content is shaped for balance

These are the shaping principles that make a run tunable rather than a pile of numbers. They constrain *how* content is authored; the resulting quantities live in RON.

### Staggered complexity as choice
Depth is introduced tier by tier as **optional yield upgrades**, never dumped in at once. The simple chain always works; the deeper chain trades steps for yield or a useful side-stream. One lever appears across the forming ladder, the research ladder, and reactivity routing — always as an *optional* deepening, so a player can brute-force the simple path at higher resource cost. This is the Nullius coherence lesson (`market/nullius.md`): a legible decision ("spend extra machines + power for +yield and a trace cross-metal, or stay simple and mine more") beats a forced wall.

- **Forming ladder.** Each forming step (direct smelt → crush → wash → plate/wire) is a tiered choice trading a step for yield or a byproduct. The cross-metal trace (washing iron yields a little copper) is the interlock hook that couples two chains. The direct chain never stops working. Authored as the T2–T4 forming nodes in `tech-tree-design.md §6bis` and `assets/tech_nodes/`.
- **Research ladder.** Each research theme's pack has multiple producing recipes; deeper input = better pts/sec at the cost of a longer chain — you *upgrade* the research line as you tier rather than cloning tier-1 analyzers. The four-theme currency model, the Material/Engineering split rule, and the yield-ladder rungs are specced in [`technical/research.md §3`](technical/research.md#3-research-pool). Numbers there are illustrative; the live values are in the recipe RON.

### Material-spine sizing
Base material count is held deliberately low (GTNH-lite, not GTNH). Depth-per-material comes from **alloys** (combine two metals → a better material) and the **form ladder**, not raw material count. The base count is capped because it feeds the research packs — more base materials = more automation burden — so the pressure points toward the middle rather than toward breadth. Per-run variance lives in the exotic track, not the base spine. (The material/form/composite vocabulary is GDD §8; this is the balance rationale for keeping the base count small.)

### Interlock over volume
The graph should generate side-streams and cross-feeds, not clean input→output lines — byproduct routing *is* the planning content, and every exotic line feeds a *different* successor system so the escape requires the whole graph (GDD §8, §12). Balance-wise this means a tuning change to one chain ripples into its cross-feeds; the sweep is how that ripple is caught.

## 5. Open Questions

| # | Item | Resolution path |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Representative numbers are unvalidated.** Yields, exit-gate quantities, fuel stockpile, node counts, successor-system input quantities were authored as plausible first-pass values. | Playtest + the `scenario balance` sweep after e2e completability; the measured baseline is `balance-state.md`. |
| 2 | **Multi-theme currency lockout.** The 4-theme research model (`research.md §3`) reverses the earlier single-currency stance — a player must never be stranded with points in a theme that unlocks nothing spendable. The mitigations (every theme always-earnable from its intro tier; theme sources overlap the main line) must be shown to hold. | Validate "no theme strandable" across the balance sweep / curated seeds; each theme's generator is unlocked by a node that does *not* cost that theme (see `research.md §3` earn-order). |
| 3 | **Tier-1→2 gate quantity** and other non-terminal exit gates are provisional theme anchors, not settled numbers. | Tracked as tech-tree progression design in `tech-tree-design.md §7 Q#1–2`; balance sweep informs the eventual quantities. |
